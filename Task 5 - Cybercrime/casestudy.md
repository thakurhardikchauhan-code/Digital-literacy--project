UPI fraud is one of the most common cybercrimes today, especially among students and new users of digital payment systems. In this type of fraud, attackers usually contact the victim through phone calls, messages, or fake links while pretending to be bank officials or trusted individuals. They often try to to create urgency & ask the user to approve a payment request or share sensitive details.

Many users get confused and believe they are receiving money, but in reality, they are authorising a transaction. Once approved, the money is deducted instantly from their account. Students and elderly people are often targeted because they may not be fully aware of such scams.

The consequences of UPI fraud include financial loss & stress. In some cases, the money cannot be recovered. This highlights the importance of awareness & understanding how digital payment systems work to avoid such situations.
